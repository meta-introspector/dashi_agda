## Executive Boundary

The repo currently supports a **typed closure-and-bridge program with explicit
theorem owners**, not a finished proof of physical unification.

What is real today:

- a canonical theorem route from the concrete closure stack through quadratic,
  signature, Clifford, and full-closure surfaces;
- an explicit unified-physics theorem owner that packages the canonical
  checklist, five-pillar theorem, full closure, bridge package, and concrete
  unification inhabitant;
- an explicit adapter theorem that connects abstract
  `UnifiedClosure.PhysicsUnification` to the canonical closure package;
- a bounded minimal-credible shift instance;
- a narrow realization-independence theorem surface contrasting the canonical
  shift witness with an independent B4-backed witness;
- theorem-bearing known-limits `grLike` and `qftLike` status surfaces;
- explicit packaging interfaces that let dynamics, variational, gradient-flow,
  and wave-facing consumers talk about the same bounded carrier;
- substantial shell/orbit/profile formalism and a theorem-bearing known-limits
  wave ladder, now packaged into an atom/chemistry recovery carrier even
  though that carrier still leaves explicit chemistry/atomic-recovery gates;
- a schedule-independent coarse natural/conserved law, plus explicit
  widening-obstruction and next-ingredient gap surfaces for that lane;
- an explicit canonical-to-noncanonical coarse recovery identification at the
  adapter seam, plus fieldwise `gauge/basin/motif` seam equalities and an
  explicit richer MDL-side blocker;
- a gauge/matter interpretable-observable theorem that now reaches the
  known-limits GR/QFT bridge owners and a downstream consumer surface;
- shell/dictionary recovery, atomic/photonuclear contact, and
  chemistry-right-limits gates, plus an explicit interpretation blocker for
  stronger chemistry claims;
- an empirical photonuclear lane that is normalized and explicitly non-claiming.

What is not real today:

- a theorem that these surfaces jointly recover all of GR and QFT;
- a realization-independent derivation from one primitive law to the full
  physical language;
- a finished gauge/matter recovery theorem;
- a natural dynamics law that removes the present engineered/local shift
  choices.

- The signature, Clifford, spin/Dirac, and known-limits GR/QFT surfaces are
  meaningful bridge layers over the canonical closure route.

  natural dynamics, conserved observables, continuum witness, transport, and

  theorem-thin consumer surfaces, not empty rhetoric.

  wave geometry / wave regime / wave observables ladder with canonical
  consumers.

## `packaging`

- `DashiDynamicsShiftInstance` packages the current shift carrier, pressure
  status, and photonuclear validation summary into one bounded interface.
- The atom/quantum-facing side already includes shell/orbit/profile
  computation, synthetic shell realization witnesses, and archive-backed
  dictionary language around closed-shell behavior, but not a finished atomic
  recovery theorem.
- The current unifying interfaces mostly assemble existing witnesses and
  bounded instances; they do not yet derive the physics they name.
- The minimal-credible closure milestone is a real package, but part of its
  force is still that it organizes the repo into a coherent canonical surface.

## `empirical`

- The photonuclear registry, measurement surface, evidence summary, validation
  summary, and HEPData normalization scripts are empirical-only.
- They support calibration, diagnostics, and future interpretation contracts.
- They are not currently evidence that the theorem stack has recovered LHC
  physics from first principles.

## `speculative`

- physical unification in the usual strong sense
- realization-independent closure
- continuum-limit law with direct physical interpretation
- full gauge/matter recovery
- a derived wave/phase/field dynamics law rather than a bounded shift program

## Current Bottleneck

The repo’s unification bottleneck is not lack of vocabulary. It is lack of a
fully promoted theorem that turns the present bounded closure stack into a more
general physical derivation.

Concretely:

- the canonical route is still strongest on contraction, projected delta,
  quadratic forcing, and bounded signature/Clifford transport;
- the present dynamics-facing surfaces remain local or engineered enough that
  they cannot yet serve as a natural universal law;
- the known-limits GR/QFT bridges should still be read as disciplined,
  theorem-bearing regime recovery notes, not as closure of the entire
  unification program.
